1960s Furniture A Guide to Iconic Styles and Bold Designs

Written by Robby Nov 13, 2023 · 17 min read

Immerse yourself in the captivating world of 1960s furniture, where vibrant colors, bold shapes, and innovative materials pushed the boundaries of interior design. This era marked a time of experimentation and liberation, as furniture designers sought to break away from traditional forms and embrace the spirit of the swinging sixties.

From the sleek and futuristic designs of Eero Saarinen to the playful and funky creations of Verner Panton, 1960s furniture exudes a unique blend of sophistication and fun. This era saw the rise of iconic pieces, such as the egg chair, the beanbag chair, and the tulip table, which continue to grace modern interiors with their timeless appeal.

Whether you're a collector seeking rare vintage finds or simply looking for inspiration for your own home decor, this guide will take you on a journey through the captivating world of 1960s furniture.

1960 Furniture

1960s furniture is characterized by its bold designs, vibrant colors, and innovative use of materials. Here are ten important points to remember about this iconic era in furniture design:

  • Organic shapes: Inspired by nature, 1960s furniture often featured curved lines and organic forms.
  • Bright colors: Furniture designers embraced vibrant hues, such as orange, yellow, and green, to reflect the optimism and energy of the decade.
  • New materials: Plastic, fiberglass, and foam were widely used, allowing for new and innovative designs.
  • Space Age influence: The Space Race inspired futuristic and streamlined designs.
  • Comfort and functionality: Furniture was designed to be both stylish and comfortable, with a focus on ergonomics.
  • Iconic pieces: The 1960s produced some of the most iconic furniture pieces of all time, such as the Egg Chair and the Tulip Table.
  • Eclecticism: 1960s furniture embraced a mix of styles, from retro to modern to ethnic.
  • Pop Art influence: The Pop Art movement influenced furniture design, with bold patterns and graphic elements.
  • Affordable design: Mass production techniques made 1960s furniture more affordable and accessible to a wider audience.
  • Legacy: 1960s furniture continues to inspire and influence contemporary design.

Whether you're a collector or simply appreciate good design, 1960s furniture offers a unique and captivating glimpse into a transformative era in interior design.

Organic shapes: Inspired by nature, 1960s furniture often featured curved lines and organic forms.

The 1960s was a time of great experimentation and innovation in furniture design. Designers sought to break away from the rigid and formal styles of the past and embrace a more organic and fluid aesthetic. Inspired by nature, they incorporated curved lines and organic forms into their designs.

This trend was evident in a wide range of furniture pieces, from chairs and sofas to tables and lamps. Curved lines created a sense of movement and fluidity, while organic forms evoked the natural world. Furniture designers used materials such as wood, plastic, and fiberglass to create pieces that were both stylish and comfortable.

One of the most iconic examples of organic furniture design from the 1960s is the Egg Chair by Arne Jacobsen. This chair features a curved fiberglass shell that envelops the sitter, creating a sense of privacy and comfort. Other notable examples include the Tulip Chair by Eero Saarinen, the Marshmallow Sofa by George Nelson, and the Womb Chair by Eero Saarinen.

Organic furniture design continues to be popular today. Many contemporary furniture designers draw inspiration from the 1960s, creating pieces that are both stylish and functional. Whether you're looking for a vintage piece or a modern take on the organic style, there are many options to choose from.

The organic shapes and curved lines of 1960s furniture create a sense of movement and fluidity that is both visually appealing and comfortable. This style is perfect for creating a warm and inviting atmosphere in your home.

Bright colors: Furniture designers embraced vibrant hues, such as orange, yellow, and green, to reflect the optimism and energy of the decade.

The 1960s was a time of great social and cultural change. The decade was marked by a sense of optimism and energy, and this was reflected in the design of the era. Furniture designers embraced vibrant colors, such as orange, yellow, and green, to create pieces that were both stylish and uplifting.

  • Orange: Orange was a popular color in the 1960s, as it was seen as a symbol of optimism and joy. Orange furniture was often used to create a cheerful and inviting atmosphere.
  • Yellow: Yellow was another popular color in the 1960s, as it was seen as a symbol of happiness and sunshine. Yellow furniture was often used to brighten up a room and create a sense of warmth.
  • Green: Green was a popular color in the 1960s, as it was seen as a symbol of nature and growth. Green furniture was often used to create a sense of calm and tranquility.
  • Other colors: In addition to orange, yellow, and green, other popular colors in the 1960s included pink, blue, and purple. These colors were often used in combination to create bold and eye-catching designs.

Bright colors were not only used on furniture, but also on other home decor items, such as wallpaper, curtains, and rugs. This created a vibrant and energetic atmosphere that was characteristic of the 1960s.

New materials: Plastic, fiberglass, and foam were widely used, allowing for new and innovative designs.

The 1960s was a time of great innovation in materials science. New materials, such as plastic, fiberglass, and foam, were developed and used in a wide range of products, including furniture. These new materials allowed furniture designers to create pieces that were both stylish and functional.

  • Plastic: Plastic was a popular material for furniture in the 1960s because it was lightweight, durable, and easy to mold into different shapes. Plastic furniture was often used in outdoor settings, as it was resistant to weather and fading.
  • Fiberglass: Fiberglass was another popular material for furniture in the 1960s. It was strong and durable, and it could be molded into complex shapes. Fiberglass furniture was often used in indoor settings, as it was easy to clean and maintain.
  • Foam: Foam was a new material that was used in furniture in the 1960s. It was lightweight and comfortable, and it could be used to create upholstered furniture with a soft and inviting look.
  • Other materials: In addition to plastic, fiberglass, and foam, other new materials that were used in furniture in the 1960s included metal, glass, and vinyl. These materials were often used in combination to create unique and stylish pieces.

The use of new materials in furniture design allowed for a greater range of possibilities. Furniture designers were no longer limited to traditional materials, such as wood and metal. They could now create pieces that were more lightweight, durable, and comfortable.

Space Age influence: The Space Race inspired futuristic and streamlined designs.

The Space Race between the United States and the Soviet Union had a profound impact on American culture, including furniture design. The race to space inspired a fascination with all things futuristic, and this was reflected in the design of furniture and other home decor items.

Furniture designers began to incorporate elements of space travel into their work. They used new materials, such as plastic and fiberglass, to create pieces that were lightweight and streamlined. They also used bold colors and geometric shapes to create a sense of futurism.

One of the most iconic examples of Space Age furniture is the Ball Chair by Eero Aarnio. This chair is made of a fiberglass shell that is suspended from the ceiling by a single wire. It looks like something out of a science fiction movie, and it perfectly captures the spirit of the Space Age.

Other notable examples of Space Age furniture include the Panton Chair by Verner Panton, the Tulip Chair by Eero Saarinen, and the Cone Chair by Arne Jacobsen. These pieces are all characterized by their clean lines, simple forms, and use of new materials.

The Space Age influence on furniture design was short-lived, but it had a lasting impact. Many of the design elements that were popular during this era, such as the use of bold colors and geometric shapes, are still used in furniture design today.

Comfort and functionality: Furniture was designed to be both stylish and comfortable, with a focus on ergonomics.

In the 1960s, furniture designers began to place a greater emphasis on comfort and functionality. They realized that furniture should not only look good, but it should also be comfortable to sit in and use. This led to the development of new ergonomic designs that were designed to support the human body.

  • Adjustable furniture: Adjustable furniture was a popular trend in the 1960s. This type of furniture allowed users to customize their seating position for maximum comfort. For example, many chairs and sofas were designed with adjustable backrests and headrests.
  • Upholstered furniture: Upholstered furniture was another popular trend in the 1960s. Upholstered furniture is more comfortable to sit in than un-upholstered furniture, and it can also add a touch of luxury to a room.
  • Ergonomic design: Ergonomic design is the study of how people interact with their environment. In the 1960s, furniture designers began to apply ergonomic principles to their work. This led to the development of furniture that was more comfortable and supportive for the human body.
  • Multi-functional furniture: Multi-functional furniture was also popular in the 1960s. This type of furniture can be used for multiple purposes, which makes it a great choice for small spaces. For example, a coffee table with built-in storage can also be used as a footrest or a side table.

The focus on comfort and functionality in furniture design led to the development of some of the most iconic furniture pieces of the 20th century. These pieces are still popular today because they are both stylish and comfortable.
