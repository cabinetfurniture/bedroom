Seventies Furniture A Guide to Retro Style

Written by Oliver Dec 30, 2024 · 10 min read

If you're a fan of vintage décor, then you'll love the seventies furniture trend. This iconic style is all about bold colors, geometric shapes, and plush textures. Whether you're looking to add a few statement pieces to your home or completely overhaul your décor, this guide will help you create a seventies-inspired space that's both stylish and comfortable.

The seventies were a time of great change and upheaval, and this is reflected in the furniture of the era. Seventies furniture is often characterized by its eclectic mix of styles, from Art Deco to Mid-Century Modern. This eclecticism can be seen in the use of different materials, such as wood, metal, and plastic, as well as in the variety of shapes and sizes.

Now that you have a basic understanding of seventies furniture, let's take a look at some of the specific pieces that you can add to your home to create a retro look.

seventies furniture

Here are five key characteristics of seventies furniture:

  • Bold colors
  • Geometric shapes
  • Plush textures
  • Eclectic mix of styles
  • Use of different materials

These characteristics can be seen in a variety of seventies furniture pieces, from sofas and chairs to tables and lamps. If you're looking to add a touch of seventies style to your home, start by incorporating some of these key elements into your décor.

Bold colors

One of the most striking features of seventies furniture is its use of bold colors. This was a time when people were not afraid to experiment with bright and vibrant hues, and this is reflected in the furniture of the era.

  • Orange: This was one of the most popular colors of the seventies, and it was used in a variety of furniture pieces, from sofas to chairs to lamps. Orange is a warm and inviting color that can add a touch of sunshine to any room.
  • Yellow: Another popular color of the seventies, yellow is a cheerful and optimistic color that can brighten up any space. Yellow furniture is perfect for adding a pop of color to a living room or bedroom.
  • Green: Green was another popular color of the seventies, and it was often used in combination with other bold colors, such as orange and yellow. Green is a versatile color that can be used to create a variety of different looks, from retro to modern.
  • Blue: Blue was also a popular color of the seventies, and it was often used in combination with white or brown. Blue is a calming and serene color that can create a relaxing atmosphere in any room.

These are just a few of the many bold colors that were used in seventies furniture. If you're looking to add a touch of retro style to your home, don't be afraid to experiment with some of these vibrant hues.

Geometric shapes

Another key characteristic of seventies furniture is its use of geometric shapes. This was a time when designers were experimenting with new and innovative forms, and this is reflected in the furniture of the era.

  • Circles: Circles were a popular shape in seventies furniture, and they were often used in combination with other geometric shapes, such as squares and rectangles. Circles can be used to create a variety of different looks, from retro to modern.
  • Squares: Squares were another popular shape in seventies furniture, and they were often used to create bold and graphic designs. Squares can be used to create a variety of different looks, from minimalist to Art Deco.
  • Rectangles: Rectangles were also a popular shape in seventies furniture, and they were often used to create clean and simple designs. Rectangles can be used to create a variety of different looks, from modern to Scandinavian.
  • Triangles: Triangles were also a popular shape in seventies furniture, and they were often used to create dynamic and exciting designs. Triangles can be used to create a variety of different looks, from retro to contemporary.

These are just a few of the many geometric shapes that were used in seventies furniture. If you're looking to add a touch of retro style to your home, don't be afraid to experiment with some of these bold and geometric shapes.

Plush textures

Seventies furniture is also known for its use of plush textures. This was a time when people were looking for furniture that was both comfortable and stylish, and this is reflected in the fabrics that were used.

Some of the most popular fabrics used in seventies furniture include:

  • Velvet: Velvet is a luxurious fabric that was often used to upholster sofas and chairs. Velvet is soft and smooth to the touch, and it adds a touch of glamour to any room.
  • Suede: Suede is another popular fabric that was used in seventies furniture. Suede is a soft and durable fabric that is perfect for creating a relaxed and inviting atmosphere.
  • Leather: Leather is a classic fabric that was often used to upholster sofas, chairs, and ottomans. Leather is durable and easy to clean, and it adds a touch of sophistication to any room.
  • Faux fur: Faux fur was also a popular fabric in the seventies, and it was often used to create cozy and inviting spaces. Faux fur is soft and warm to the touch, and it adds a touch of luxury to any room.

These are just a few of the many plush textures that were used in seventies furniture. If you're looking to add a touch of retro style to your home, don't be afraid to experiment with some of these luxurious fabrics.

In addition to the fabrics used, seventies furniture is also known for its use of tufting and other decorative techniques. Tufting is a process of creating a raised pattern in fabric, and it was often used to add a touch of visual interest to sofas and chairs. Other decorative techniques that were popular in the seventies include quilting, embroidery, and appliqué.

Eclectic mix of styles

One of the most defining characteristics of seventies furniture is its eclectic mix of styles. This was a time when people were not afraid to experiment with different styles, and this is reflected in the furniture of the era.

  • Art Deco: Art Deco was a popular style in the 1920s and 1930s, and it was revived in the seventies. Art Deco furniture is characterized by its clean lines, geometric shapes, and luxurious materials.
  • Mid-Century Modern: Mid-Century Modern was a popular style in the 1950s and 1960s, and it was also popular in the seventies. Mid-Century Modern furniture is characterized by its organic shapes, tapered legs, and use of natural materials.
  • Retro: Retro furniture is furniture that is inspired by past styles. In the seventies, this meant furniture that was inspired by the 1950s and 1960s. Retro furniture is often characterized by its bright colors, bold patterns, and geometric shapes.
  • Bohemian: Bohemian furniture is characterized by its eclectic mix of styles and materials. Bohemian furniture often includes pieces from different cultures and eras. It is often colorful and eclectic.

These are just a few of the many styles that were popular in the seventies. When it comes to seventies furniture, there are no rules. You can mix and match different styles to create a look that is uniquely your own.

Use of different materials

Another key characteristic of seventies furniture is its use of different materials. This was a time when manufacturers were not afraid to experiment with new materials, and this is reflected in the furniture of the era.

  • Wood: Wood was the most popular material used in seventies furniture. Wood furniture is durable and versatile, and it can be used to create a variety of different looks.
  • Metal: Metal was also a popular material used in seventies furniture. Metal furniture is durable and easy to clean, and it can add a touch of industrial style to any room.
  • Plastic: Plastic was another popular material used in seventies furniture. Plastic furniture is lightweight and affordable, and it can be used to create a variety of different colors and shapes.
  • Glass: Glass was also a popular material used in seventies furniture. Glass furniture is elegant and sophisticated, and it can add a touch of glamour to any room.

These are just a few of the many different materials that were used in seventies furniture. When it comes to seventies furniture, there are no rules. You can mix and match different materials to create a look that is uniquely your own.
