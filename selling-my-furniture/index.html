Selling Your Furniture A Guide to Maximizing Profits

Written by Daniel Jun 19, 2025 Â· 15 min read

Are you looking to declutter your home and make some extra cash? Selling your used furniture is a great way to do both. But before you start listing your items online or in classified ads, there are a few things you need to do to ensure you get the most money for your pieces.

In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about selling your furniture, from preparing your items to negotiating with buyers. We'll also provide tips on how to stage your furniture to make it look its best and how to use online marketplaces to reach a wider audience.

Selling Your Furniture

Before you start selling your furniture, there are a few important things to keep in mind:

  • Clean and repair: Make sure your furniture is clean and in good condition before listing it for sale.
  • Take good photos: Take clear, well-lit photos of your furniture from all angles.
  • Write a detailed description: Include all relevant information about your furniture, such as the dimensions, materials, and condition.
  • Price it competitively: Research similar furniture online to get an idea of what it's worth.
  • Use multiple platforms: List your furniture on multiple online marketplaces and classified ads websites.
  • Be patient: It may take some time to sell your furniture, so don't get discouraged.
  • Negotiate with buyers: Be prepared to negotiate with buyers, but don't give away your furniture for less than it's worth.
  • Deliver the furniture safely: If you're delivering the furniture yourself, make sure to do so safely and securely.

By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a good price.

Clean and repair: Make sure your furniture is clean and in good condition before listing it for sale.

Before you list your furniture for sale, it's important to clean it thoroughly and make any necessary repairs. This will make your furniture more appealing to buyers and help you get a better price.

To clean your furniture, start by dusting it with a soft cloth. Then, use a mild soap and water solution to wipe down the surfaces. Be sure to dry your furniture thoroughly with a clean cloth.

If your furniture has any stains, you can try to remove them with a stain remover. Test the stain remover on a small area first to make sure it doesn't damage the fabric or finish.

If your furniture has any tears or holes, you can repair them with a needle and thread or a fabric patch. If the damage is more extensive, you may need to take your furniture to a professional upholsterer.

Once your furniture is clean and repaired, it's ready to be listed for sale. By taking the time to clean and repair your furniture, you can increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a good price.

Take good photos: Take clear, well-lit photos of your furniture from all angles.

One of the most important things you can do to sell your furniture quickly and for a good price is to take good photos. Clear, well-lit photos will help buyers see the condition of your furniture and make them more likely to purchase it.

  • Take photos in natural light. Natural light will give your photos a more flattering look than artificial light. If possible, take your photos outside on a cloudy day or near a window on a sunny day.
  • Use a high-quality camera. The better the quality of your camera, the better your photos will look. If you don't have a high-quality camera, you can use your smartphone, but make sure to use the highest resolution setting.
  • Take photos from all angles. Buyers want to see your furniture from all sides, so be sure to take photos from the front, back, sides, and top. You should also take close-up photos of any unique features or details.
  • Edit your photos. Once you've taken your photos, you can edit them to make them look their best. You can crop the photos to remove any unnecessary background, adjust the brightness and contrast, and sharpen the image.

By following these tips, you can take great photos of your furniture that will help you sell it quickly and for a good price.

Write a detailed description: Include all relevant information about your furniture, such as the dimensions, materials, and condition.

When you're writing a description of your furniture, be sure to include all relevant information, such as the dimensions, materials, and condition. This will help buyers make an informed decision about whether or not to purchase your furniture.

  • Dimensions: Include the length, width, and height of your furniture. If your furniture has multiple pieces, such as a couch and ottoman, be sure to list the dimensions of each piece.
  • Materials: List the materials that your furniture is made of, such as wood, metal, fabric, or leather. If your furniture is made of multiple materials, be sure to list all of them.
  • Condition: Describe the condition of your furniture. Is it new, used, or antique? Are there any scratches, dents, or tears? Be honest about the condition of your furniture so that buyers know what to expect.
  • Other relevant information: In addition to the dimensions, materials, and condition, you may also want to include other relevant information about your furniture, such as the style, brand, or age. You can also mention any unique features or details that make your furniture special.

By providing buyers with a detailed description of your furniture, you can help them make an informed decision about whether or not to purchase it. This will increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a good price.

Price it competitively: Research similar furniture online to get an idea of what it's worth.

One of the most important factors in selling your furniture quickly and for a good price is to price it competitively. If you price your furniture too high, buyers will be less likely to purchase it. If you price your furniture too low, you'll end up losing money.

The best way to price your furniture competitively is to research similar furniture online. Look for furniture that is similar in style, size, and condition to your furniture. Once you've found a few comparable pieces, you can get an idea of what your furniture is worth.

When pricing your furniture, be sure to consider the following factors:

  • The condition of your furniture: Furniture that is in good condition will sell for more than furniture that is damaged or worn.
  • The style of your furniture: Some styles of furniture are more popular than others. If your furniture is in a popular style, you may be able to sell it for more money.
  • The age of your furniture: Antique furniture can sell for more money than newer furniture. However, if your furniture is too old, it may be difficult to sell.
  • The location of your furniture: The price of furniture can vary depending on where you live. If you live in a large city, you may be able to sell your furniture for more money than if you live in a small town.

Once you've considered all of these factors, you can price your furniture competitively. By pricing your furniture correctly, you can increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a good price.

Use multiple platforms: List your furniture on multiple online marketplaces and classified ads websites.

One of the best ways to sell your furniture quickly and for a good price is to list it on multiple online marketplaces and classified ads websites. This will give your furniture more exposure and increase your chances of finding a buyer.

Here are some of the most popular online marketplaces and classified ads websites for selling furniture:

  • Facebook Marketplace: Facebook Marketplace is a great place to sell furniture because it has a large audience of potential buyers. You can list your furniture for free and reach people in your local area.
  • Craigslist: Craigslist is another popular option for selling furniture. It's free to list your furniture and you can reach people in your local area or across the country.
  • eBay: eBay is a good option for selling furniture if you're willing to ship it. You can list your furniture for a fixed price or auction it off to the highest bidder.
  • Chairish: Chairish is a specialized marketplace for selling high-end furniture. If you have high-quality furniture to sell, Chairish is a good option.
  • AptDeco: AptDeco is a specialized marketplace for selling furniture in New York City. If you live in New York City, AptDeco is a good option.

When listing your furniture on multiple platforms, be sure to use high-quality photos and write detailed descriptions. You should also price your furniture competitively. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a good price.

Be patient: It may take some time to sell your furniture, so don't get discouraged.

Selling furniture can take time, so it's important to be patient. Don't get discouraged if your furniture doesn't sell right away. Keep your listings active and continue to promote your furniture on social media and other online platforms.

Here are a few tips for staying patient while selling your furniture:

  • Set realistic expectations. Don't expect your furniture to sell overnight. It may take weeks or even months to find a buyer, especially if you're selling high-priced or specialized furniture.
  • Price your furniture competitively. If your furniture is priced too high, buyers will be less likely to purchase it. Do your research to find out what similar furniture is selling for and price your furniture accordingly.
  • Promote your furniture. List your furniture on multiple online marketplaces and classified ads websites. You can also promote your furniture on social media and other online platforms.
  • Be patient and persistent. Don't give up if your furniture doesn't sell right away. Keep your listings active and continue to promote your furniture. Eventually, you will find a buyer.

Selling furniture can be a slow process, but it's important to be patient and persistent. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your furniture quickly and for a good price.

Negotiate with buyers: Be prepared to negotiate with buyers, but don't give away your furniture for less than it's worth.

When you're selling furniture, it's important to be prepared to negotiate with buyers. However, you should never give away your furniture for less than it's worth. Here are a few tips for negotiating with buyers:

  • Be prepared to walk away. If a buyer is not willing to pay your asking price, be prepared to walk away. It's better to wait for a buyer who is willing to pay what your furniture is worth than to sell it for less than you want.
  • Be willing to compromise. You may not get your full asking price, but you should be willing to compromise. Be prepared to meet the buyer halfway, but don't give away your furniture for less than you're willing to accept.
  • Be firm but polite. When negotiating with buyers, be firm but polite. Stand your ground and don't let the buyer pressure you into selling your furniture for less than you want.
  • Get everything in writing. Once you've reached an agreement with a buyer, be sure to get everything in writing. This will protect you in case there are any disputes later on.

Negotiating with buyers can be tricky, but it's important to remember that you're in control of the sale. Don't be afraid to walk away if a buyer is not willing to pay your asking price. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of getting a fair price for your furniture.

Deliver the furniture safely: If you're delivering the furniture yourself, make sure to do so safely and securely.

If you're delivering the furniture yourself, it's important to do so safely and securely. This will protect you and your furniture from damage.

  • Use proper lifting techniques. When lifting heavy furniture, be sure to use proper lifting techniques to avoid injury. Lift with your legs, not your back, and keep your back straight.
  • Secure the furniture in your vehicle. When transporting furniture in your vehicle, be sure to secure it properly. Use straps or bungee cords to keep the furniture from moving around and damaging itself or your vehicle.
  • Be careful when moving the furniture into your home. When moving furniture into your home, be careful not to damage your walls, floors, or furniture. Use a dolly or hand truck to move heavy furniture, and be sure to measure doorways and hallways to make sure the furniture will fit through.
  • Assemble the furniture carefully. If the furniture needs to be assembled, be sure to follow the instructions carefully. Use the correct tools and hardware, and don't overtighten the screws or bolts.

By following these tips, you can deliver your furniture safely and securely.
