Arts and Crafts Period Furniture A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Jack Nov 15, 2023 Â· 12 min read

The Arts and Crafts period, which spanned the late 19th and early 20th centuries, was a significant era in the history of furniture design. This era was characterized by a rejection of the overly ornate and mass-produced furniture of the Victorian era and a return to simpler, handcrafted forms. Arts and Crafts furniture is known for its emphasis on functionality, quality materials, and craftsmanship.

The Arts and Crafts movement was founded on the belief that art and beauty should be accessible to everyone, not just the wealthy elite. This led to a focus on creating furniture that was both aesthetically pleasing and affordable. Many Arts and Crafts furniture pieces were made by hand by skilled craftsmen, using traditional techniques and tools. The resulting furniture was often simple in design, with clean lines and minimal ornamentation.

In the following sections, we will explore the key characteristics of Arts and Crafts period furniture, discuss the different types of furniture that were produced during this era, and provide tips for identifying and collecting Arts and Crafts furniture pieces.

Simple, handcrafted forms

One of the defining characteristics of Arts and Crafts period furniture is its simple, handcrafted forms. This reflects the movement's emphasis on functionality and a rejection of the overly ornate and mass-produced furniture of the Victorian era.

Arts and Crafts furniture makers often drew inspiration from traditional folk art and vernacular furniture forms. They believed that these simple forms were more honest and authentic than the elaborate designs of the Victorian era. They also believed that simple forms were more functional and practical for everyday use.

As a result, Arts and Crafts furniture is often characterized by clean lines, geometric shapes, and minimal ornamentation. This simplicity of form allowed the beauty of the natural materials used in construction to shine through.

Arts and Crafts furniture makers also paid great attention to craftsmanship. They used traditional hand-crafting techniques to create furniture that was both beautiful and durable. This attention to detail is evident in the fine joinery, carved details, and hand-hammered hardware that are often found on Arts and Crafts furniture.

The simple, handcrafted forms of Arts and Crafts furniture are a reflection of the movement's emphasis on functionality, honesty, and beauty. This furniture is designed to be both useful and beautiful, and it is made to last for generations.

Natural materials

Arts and Crafts furniture makers believed that natural materials were essential for creating beautiful and durable furniture. They favored woods with natural grain patterns, such as oak, maple, and cherry. They also used other natural materials, such as leather, copper, and stone.

Arts and Crafts furniture makers often left the natural finish of the wood exposed, or they used simple stains and finishes to enhance the wood's natural beauty. This allowed the grain and texture of the wood to shine through.

The use of natural materials in Arts and Crafts furniture is a reflection of the movement's emphasis on honesty and simplicity. Arts and Crafts furniture makers believed that natural materials were more honest and authentic than artificial materials. They also believed that natural materials were more beautiful and durable.

The use of natural materials also helped to create a sense of unity between the furniture and the natural world. Arts and Crafts furniture is often designed to blend in with its surroundings, rather than standing out as a separate object.

The use of natural materials is one of the things that makes Arts and Crafts furniture so appealing. Natural materials are warm and inviting, and they create a sense of connection to the natural world. Arts and Crafts furniture is also very durable, as natural materials are strong and long-lasting.

Clean lines

Arts and Crafts furniture is characterized by its clean lines. This means that the furniture is free of unnecessary ornamentation and has a simple, geometric form.

  • Straight lines and simple curves

    Arts and Crafts furniture often features straight lines and simple curves. This creates a sense of order and harmony.

  • Minimal ornamentation

    Arts and Crafts furniture is typically free of unnecessary ornamentation. This allows the beauty of the natural materials used in construction to shine through.

  • Geometric shapes

    Arts and Crafts furniture often incorporates geometric shapes, such as squares, rectangles, and circles. These shapes create a sense of balance and unity.

  • Emphasis on negative space

    Arts and Crafts furniture makers often used negative space to create a sense of lightness and airiness. This can be seen in the open slats of a chair back or the space between the legs of a table.

The clean lines of Arts and Crafts furniture are a reflection of the movement's emphasis on simplicity and functionality. Arts and Crafts furniture makers believed that furniture should be simple, practical, and beautiful. They also believed that furniture should be made to last for generations.

Affordable and accessible

One of the goals of the Arts and Crafts movement was to make art and beauty accessible to everyone, not just the wealthy elite. This led to a focus on creating furniture that was both affordable and accessible.

  • Simple design

    Arts and Crafts furniture is often simple in design, which makes it less expensive to produce.

  • Use of local materials

    Arts and Crafts furniture makers often used local materials, which are typically less expensive than imported materials.

  • Hand-crafted construction

    While hand-crafted furniture is more expensive than mass-produced furniture, it is still more affordable than furniture that is made entirely by machine.

  • Sold through cooperatives

    Arts and Crafts furniture was often sold through cooperatives, which allowed furniture makers to sell their furniture directly to consumers, without the need for a middleman.

As a result of these factors, Arts and Crafts furniture was more affordable and accessible than other types of furniture that were available at the time. This made it possible for people from all walks of life to own beautiful and well-made furniture.
