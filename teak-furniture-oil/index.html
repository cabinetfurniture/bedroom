Teak oil is a natural product derived from the leaves of the teak tree (Tectona grandis). It is commonly used as a wood finish, providing protection and enhancing the natural beauty of the wood grain. In this article, we will delve into the properties, benefits, and applications of teak oil.

Properties of Teak Oil:

Teak oil is a highly durable and water-repellent substance. It penetrates deeply into the wood, forming a protective barrier against moisture, UV rays, and environmental pollutants. Its rich golden-brown color adds a warm and elegant touch to the wood surface.

Protects against moisture

Teak furniture oil is highly effective in protecting wood from moisture damage. Moisture can cause wood to rot, warp, and crack. Teak furniture oil penetrates deeply into the wood, creating a water-repellent barrier that prevents moisture from penetrating the wood's surface.

  • Prevents water absorption: Teak furniture oil fills the pores of the wood, preventing water from being absorbed. This is especially important for outdoor furniture, which is exposed to rain and other moisture.
  • Prevents swelling and warping: When wood absorbs moisture, it can swell and warp. Teak furniture oil prevents this by keeping the wood dry.
  • Prevents rot and decay: Moisture can cause wood to rot and decay. Teak furniture oil prevents this by creating a barrier that prevents moisture from reaching the wood's interior.
