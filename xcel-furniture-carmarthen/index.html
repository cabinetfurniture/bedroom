FAQ

We have compiled a list of frequently asked questions (FAQs) about garden furniture to help you make an informed decision before purchasing.

Question 1: What are the different types of garden furniture?
Garden furniture is typically made from a variety of materials such as wood, metal, plastic or rattan. The type of material you choose will depend on your personal preferences and the climate you live in.

Question 2: What size garden furniture should I buy?
The size of garden furniture you buy will depend on the size of your outdoor space and how many people you plan on seating. It is important to measure your space before purchasing furniture to make sure that it will fit comfortably.

Question 3: How do I care for my garden furniture?
The best way to care for your garden furniture is to clean it regularly with soap and water. You may also want to apply a sealant or wax to help protect it from the elements.

Question 4: How do I store my garden furniture during the winter?
If you live in a climate with cold winters, it is important to store your garden furniture indoors or cover it with a tarp to protect it from the elements.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outside all year round?
Whether or not you can leave your garden furniture outside all year round will depend on the climate you live in. If you live in a climate with mild winters, you may be able to leave your furniture outside all year round. However, if you live in a climate with cold winters, it is best to store your furniture indoors or cover it with a tarp to protect it from the elements.

Question 6: How do I assemble my garden furniture?
Most garden furniture is easy to assemble. Simply follow the instructions that came with your furniture. If you need help, you can always contact the manufacturer.

Question 7: How do I choose the right cushions for my garden furniture?
When choosing cushions for your garden furniture, it is important to consider the style of your furniture, the climate you live in, and how often you plan on using the cushions. If you live in a climate with heavy rainfall, you may want to choose cushions that are water-resistant or waterproof.
