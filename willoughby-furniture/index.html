Durable construction

Another important factor to consider when choosing furniture is the durability of the construction. Willoughby Furniture uses traditional joinery techniques to ensure that its furniture is built to last. These techniques include:

  • Mortise and tenon joints

    Mortise and tenon joints are strong and durable joints that have been used for centuries. They are created by cutting a mortise (a square hole) into one piece of wood and a tenon (a square peg) into another piece of wood. The tenon is then inserted into the mortise, and the joint is secured with a wooden peg or dowel.

  • Dovetail joints

    Dovetail joints are another strong and durable joint that is often used in furniture construction. They are created by cutting a series of interlocking triangles into two pieces of wood. The triangles are then fitted together, and the joint is secured with a wooden peg or dowel.

  • Corner blocks

    Corner blocks are used to reinforce the corners of furniture frames. They are typically made of wood or metal, and they are attached to the inside of the frame with screws or nails. Corner blocks help to prevent the frame from racking (twisting or warping), and they also add strength to the overall construction.

  • Plywood panels

    Plywood panels are often used in the construction of furniture backs and bottoms. Plywood is a strong and durable material that is made from thin layers of wood that are glued together. Plywood panels are resistant to warping and cracking, and they also help to reduce noise.

Willoughby Furniture's durable construction ensures that your furniture will last for years to come. Our furniture is built to withstand the rigors of everyday use, and it will continue to look great for years to come.
