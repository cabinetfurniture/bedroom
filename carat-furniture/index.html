2. Protect from Weather:
Protect your garden furniture from harsh weather conditions, such as excessive sunlight, rain, and snow. Cover the furniture with weather-resistant covers when not in use. If possible, store furniture indoors during extreme weather events or during the off-season to prolong its lifespan.

3. Maintain Regularly:
Regular maintenance is key to keeping your garden furniture in top condition. Inspect the furniture periodically for any signs of damage, such as loose screws, cracks, or rust. Accessories can enhance the comfort and style of your garden furniture. Add comfortable cushions, throws, and outdoor rugs to create a cozy and inviting atmosphere. Choose accessories that complement the overall design of your outdoor space and that are suitable for outdoor use.
