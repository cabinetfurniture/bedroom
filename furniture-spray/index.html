Tips

Practical Tips for Maintaining Garden Furniture

Beyond using furniture spray, there are several practical tips you can follow to keep your garden furniture looking its best and extend its lifespan:

Tip 1: Regular Cleaning:
Regularly cleaning your garden furniture is essential for removing dirt, debris, and stains. Use a mild detergent and warm water, avoiding harsh chemicals that could damage the furniture's finish.

Tip 2: Avoid Extreme Weather Conditions:
As much as possible, protect your garden furniture from extreme weather conditions such as heavy rain, strong winds, and intense sunlight. Consider using a furniture cover or storing the furniture in a sheltered area when not in use.

Tip 3: Proper Storage:
During the off-season or when not in use, store your garden furniture in a dry and protected location. This will help prevent damage from moisture, pests, and extreme temperatures.

Tip 4: Inspect and Tighten Hardware:
Periodically inspect your garden furniture for loose screws, bolts, or other hardware. Tighten any loose connections promptly to maintain the furniture's structural integrity and prevent accidents.
