Made Outlet The Ultimate Guide for Enhancing Your Home Connectivity

Written by Robby Oct 31, 2023 Â· 10 min read

In today's digital age, having a reliable and efficient home network is essential for seamless connectivity. Made outlets play a crucial role in delivering this enhanced connectivity experience by integrating multiple functionalities into a single, sleek device.

Made outlets combine the convenience of traditional electrical outlets with advanced networking capabilities, creating a versatile and user-friendly solution. They come equipped with Ethernet ports, Wi-Fi extenders, surge protection, and USB charging ports, allowing you to connect multiple devices while ensuring their protection and optimal performance.

Made Outlet

Made outlets offer a range of benefits that make them an ideal choice for enhancing home connectivity:

  • Ethernet Connectivity: Connect wired devices directly to the network.
  • Wi-Fi Extension: Boost Wi-Fi coverage and eliminate dead zones.
  • Surge Protection: Protect devices from power surges and voltage spikes.
  • USB Charging: Charge multiple devices conveniently with built-in USB ports.
  • Space-Saving: Combine multiple functionalities into a single outlet, reducing clutter.
  • Easy Installation: Plug and play design for hassle-free setup.

By incorporating these features, made outlets provide a comprehensive solution for optimizing home network connectivity, device charging, and safety.

Ethernet Connectivity: Connect Wired Devices Directly to the Network

Ethernet connectivity is a key feature of made outlets, enabling you to connect wired devices such as gaming consoles, smart TVs, and desktop computers directly to your home network. This wired connection provides several advantages over Wi-Fi, including:

  • Faster speeds: Ethernet connections offer significantly faster data transfer rates compared to Wi-Fi, ensuring smooth online gaming, video streaming, and file downloads.
  • Reduced latency: Ethernet connections have lower latency than Wi-Fi, resulting in more responsive gaming and real-time applications.
  • Reliability: Wired connections are less prone to interference and signal drops, providing a more stable and reliable network experience.

Made outlets typically feature one or more Ethernet ports, allowing you to connect multiple wired devices simultaneously. This is particularly beneficial for devices that require a stable and high-speed connection, such as gaming consoles or media servers.

To use the Ethernet connectivity provided by made outlets, simply connect an Ethernet cable from the outlet's Ethernet port to the Ethernet port on your device. Once connected, your device will have a direct and secure connection to your home network, providing optimal performance for wired applications.

Overall, the Ethernet connectivity offered by made outlets is a valuable feature that enhances the functionality of your home network by allowing you to connect wired devices directly for faster, more reliable, and lower latency connections.

Wi-Fi Extension: Boost Wi-Fi Coverage and Eliminate Dead Zones

Wi-Fi extension is another important feature of made outlets, allowing you to expand the coverage of your existing Wi-Fi network and eliminate dead zones where Wi-Fi signals are weak or unavailable.

Made outlets with Wi-Fi extension capabilities typically have a built-in Wi-Fi extender or repeater. These devices receive the Wi-Fi signal from your main router and rebroadcast it, creating a stronger and more reliable signal in areas where the original signal is weak.

To use the Wi-Fi extension feature of made outlets, simply plug the outlet into a power outlet within range of your main router. The outlet will automatically connect to your Wi-Fi network and begin extending the signal. Some made outlets may require additional configuration through a mobile app or web interface.

By using made outlets with Wi-Fi extension, you can effectively eliminate dead zones in your home and ensure that all your devices have a strong and stable Wi-Fi connection. This is particularly useful for large homes or homes with thick walls or other obstacles that can interfere with Wi-Fi signals.

Overall, the Wi-Fi extension feature of made outlets is a convenient and effective way to improve the coverage and reliability of your home Wi-Fi network, ensuring that all your devices can stay connected and perform optimally.

Surge Protection: Protect Devices from Power Surges and Voltage Spikes

Surge protection is a crucial feature of made outlets, safeguarding your valuable electronic devices from damage caused by power surges and voltage spikes.

  • What are power surges and voltage spikes?

    Power surges are sudden increases in voltage that can occur due to various factors such as lightning strikes, downed power lines, or faulty electrical equipment. Voltage spikes are similar to power surges, but they are typically shorter in duration and can be caused by switching on high-powered appliances or turning off inductive loads.

  • How do made outlets protect against power surges and voltage spikes?

    Made outlets with surge protection contain built-in surge protectors that divert excess voltage away from your devices and safely ground it. These surge protectors typically use metal oxide varistors (MOVs) or other surge suppression components to absorb and dissipate the surge energy.

  • What devices should I connect to surge-protected outlets?

    It is recommended to connect all sensitive electronic devices to surge-protected outlets, including computers, TVs, gaming consoles, home theater systems, and other valuable electronics. Surge protection helps prevent damage to these devices caused by sudden voltage fluctuations.

  • Are all made outlets surge-protected?

    Not all made outlets offer surge protection. When choosing a made outlet, be sure to look for models that specifically mention surge protection in their features.

By using made outlets with surge protection, you can give your electronic devices an added layer of protection against power surges and voltage spikes, ensuring their longevity and performance.

USB Charging: Charge Multiple Devices Conveniently with Built-in USB Ports

Made outlets with built-in USB ports provide a convenient way to charge your mobile devices, tablets, and other USB-powered gadgets directly from the outlet.

These USB ports typically support standard USB charging protocols, such as USB-A and USB-C, and can deliver varying amounts of power depending on the outlet model. Some made outlets offer fast charging capabilities, allowing you to quickly power up your devices.

To charge your devices using a made outlet with USB ports, simply connect your device's charging cable to one of the available USB ports on the outlet. The outlet will automatically begin charging your device, and you can continue to use the outlet's other features, such as Ethernet connectivity or surge protection.

Having multiple USB ports on a made outlet eliminates the need for separate charging adapters and reduces cable clutter. It also provides a convenient charging solution for guests or in areas where there are limited power outlets available.

When choosing a made outlet with USB ports, consider the number of ports you need, the desired charging speed, and the compatibility of the ports with your devices.

Space-Saving: Combine Multiple Functionalities into a Single Outlet, Reducing Clutter

Made outlets are designed to be space-saving, combining multiple functionalities into a single, compact device. This helps reduce cable clutter and frees up valuable space in your home or office.

Traditional power strips and extension cords often require multiple outlets and cables to provide the same functionality as a made outlet. With a made outlet, you can eliminate the need for these additional devices, as it integrates various features into a single unit.

For example, a made outlet may combine Ethernet connectivity, Wi-Fi extension, surge protection, USB charging ports, and a standard power outlet into a single device. This eliminates the need for separate Ethernet cables, Wi-Fi extenders, surge protectors, and USB chargers, resulting in a cleaner and more organized setup.

The space-saving design of made outlets is particularly beneficial in areas where space is limited, such as behind furniture, under desks, or in small apartments. By consolidating multiple functionalities into a single outlet, you can keep your cables organized and maintain a clutter-free environment.

Easy Installation: Plug and Play Design for Hassle-Free Setup

Made outlets are designed for easy installation, featuring a plug-and-play design that requires minimal effort and technical knowledge.

To install a made outlet, simply follow these steps:

  1. Unpack the made outlet and locate a suitable power outlet.
  2. Plug the made outlet into the power outlet.
  3. Connect your devices to the made outlet's Ethernet ports, USB ports, or standard power outlets as needed.

Most made outlets do not require any additional configuration or software installation. Once plugged in, they will automatically start providing the desired functionality, such as Ethernet connectivity, Wi-Fi extension, surge protection, or USB charging.

The plug-and-play design of made outlets makes them ideal for users of all skill levels. You can easily add these devices to your home or office network without the need for professional assistance or complex setup procedures.
