Dovetail joinery

Dovetail joinery is a woodworking technique that involves interlocking the edges of two pieces of wood to create a strong and durable joint. The name "dovetail" comes from the shape of the joint, which resembles the tail of a dove. Dovetail joints are often used in furniture making, as they are both strong and aesthetically pleasing.

Annaghmore Furniture is renowned for its use of dovetail joinery. The company's skilled craftsmen and craftswomen take great care in cutting and fitting each dovetail, ensuring a perfect fit that will last for generations. Dovetail joints are used in a variety of Annaghmore Furniture pieces, including tables, chairs, and cabinets.

There are several advantages to using dovetail joinery. First, dovetail joints are very strong. The interlocking design of the joint distributes stress evenly, making it resistant to pulling apart. Second, dovetail joints are durable. The tight fit of the joint prevents moisture and dirt from getting inside, which can weaken the joint over time. Third, dovetail joints are aesthetically pleasing. The intricate design of the joint adds a touch of elegance to any piece of furniture.

Dovetail joinery is a testament to the skill and dedication of Annaghmore Furniture's craftsmen and craftswomen. Each dovetail joint is a work of art, and it is this attention to detail that sets Annaghmore Furniture apart from other furniture makers.
