FAQ

Welcome to Cooke's Furniture's Garden Furniture FAQ section!

We understand that choosing the right garden furniture can be a daunting task, so we've compiled a list of frequently asked questions to help you make an informed decision.

Question 1: What factors should I consider when choosing garden furniture?

Answer: When choosing garden furniture, it's important to consider the following factors:

  • Durability: Choose furniture made from durable materials that can withstand the elements.
  • Comfort: Make sure the furniture is comfortable to sit or lie on.
  • Style: Choose furniture that matches your personal style and the overall design of your garden.
  • Functionality: Consider the functionality of the furniture. Do you need a dining set, a lounge set, or a combination of both?
  • Space: Measure your garden space to ensure that the furniture you choose fits comfortably.
  • Budget: Set a budget for your garden furniture and stick to it.

Question 2: What are the different types of garden furniture materials?

Answer: The most common types of garden furniture materials include:

  • Wood: Wood is a classic choice for garden furniture. It is durable, sturdy, and can be stained or painted to match your desired style.
  • Metal: Metal furniture is also durable and sturdy. It is often lightweight and easy to move around.
  • Plastic: Plastic furniture is lightweight, affordable, and easy to maintain. It is also available in a wide range of colors and styles.
  • Rattan: Rattan furniture is made from the stems of the rattan palm. It is lightweight, durable, and has a natural look.
  • Wicker: Wicker furniture is made from the stems of the willow tree. It is lightweight, durable, and has a classic look.

Question 7: How can I care for my garden furniture?

Answer: To care for your garden furniture, you should:

  • Clean it regularly: Use a mild detergent and water to clean your furniture. Avoid using harsh chemicals.
  • Protect it from the elements: Cover your furniture when it is not in use. If your furniture is made of wood, apply a sealant to protect it from the sun and rain.
  • Store it properly: During the winter months, store your furniture in a dry, protected place.

Tips

Follow these practical tips to choose and care for your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right material:

When choosing garden furniture, select a material that is durable and can withstand the elements in your area. Consider factors such as sunlight, rain, and humidity. Some popular materials for garden furniture include teak, aluminum, and wicker.

Tip 2: Measure your space:

Before you start shopping for garden furniture, measure your outdoor space to determine how much furniture you can comfortably fit. This will help you avoid overcrowding your garden and ensure that you have enough space to move around comfortably.

Tip 3: Choose comfortable furniture:

Garden furniture should be comfortable to sit or lie on, especially if you plan on spending long periods of time outdoors. Look for furniture with cushions and adjustable features to ensure maximum comfort.

Tip 4: Protect your furniture:

To extend the lifespan of your garden furniture, protect it from the elements when it is not in use. Use covers or store your furniture in a dry, protected place during the winter months. You can also apply a sealant to wooden furniture to protect it from the sun and rain.

By following these tips, you can choose and care for garden furniture that will provide you with years of enjoyment.
