If you're looking for a decorating style that exudes warmth, coziness, and a touch of nostalgia, cottage style furniture might be the perfect choice for you. With its simple lines, natural materials, and charming details, cottage style furniture can transform your home into a cozy retreat.

Cottage style furniture is often made from light-colored woods such as pine, oak, or maple. The pieces are typically simple in design, with clean lines and minimal ornamentation. Distressed finishes and antique hardware are also common features of cottage style furniture, giving it a timeless appeal.

Whether you're furnishing a cozy cottage in the countryside or adding a touch of charm to your suburban home, cottage style furniture can help you create a space that feels both inviting and stylish.

Simple lines

Cottage style furniture is characterized by its simple lines. This means that the pieces are typically unadorned and have a clean, classic look. The focus is on the natural beauty of the materials and the overall shape of the piece, rather than on elaborate ornamentation.

Simple lines are a key element of cottage style because they create a sense of peace and tranquility. The eye is not drawn to a lot of fussy details, so you can relax and enjoy the overall ambiance of the room. Simple lines also make it easy to mix and match different pieces of furniture, creating a unique and personal look for your home.

Some tips for incorporating simple lines into your cottage style furniture:

  • Choose pieces with clean, straight lines and minimal ornamentation.
  • Look for furniture made from natural materials, such as wood, metal, or stone.
  • Avoid pieces with a lot of intricate carvings or embellishments.
  • Mix and match different pieces of furniture to create a unique and personal look.

By following these tips, you can create a cottage style home that is both stylish and relaxing.

Natural materials

Cottage style furniture is often made from natural materials, such as wood, metal, and stone. These materials bring a sense of warmth and coziness to a space, and they also have a timeless appeal.

  • Wood: Wood is a classic choice for cottage style furniture. It is durable, versatile, and has a natural beauty that can't be replicated by other materials. Pine, oak, and maple are all popular choices for cottage style furniture.
  • Metal: Metal is another popular choice for cottage style furniture. It is strong, durable, and can be easily painted or distressed to create a unique look. Wrought iron and cast iron are both common types of metal used in cottage style furniture.
  • Stone: Stone is a beautiful and durable material that can add a touch of elegance to a cottage style home. Granite, marble, and slate are all popular choices for cottage style furniture.
  • Other natural materials: In addition to wood, metal, and stone, other natural materials can also be used to create cottage style furniture. These materials include rattan, wicker, and seagrass. These materials add a touch of texture and interest to a space, and they are also very durable.

By using natural materials in your cottage style furniture, you can create a space that is both stylish and inviting. Natural materials are also a good choice for people who are looking for sustainable and eco-friendly furniture options.

Distressed finishes

Distressed finishes are a popular way to add character and charm to cottage style furniture. Distressing is a process that intentionally creates a worn and aged look, giving the furniture a sense of history and authenticity.

There are many different ways to distress furniture. Some common techniques include:

  • Sanding: Sanding the furniture with a medium-grit sandpaper will remove the top layer of finish and create a rough, aged look.
  • Chipping: Use a hammer or other blunt object to chip away at the paint or finish, creating small nicks and scratches.
  • Scraping: Use a sharp object to scrape away the paint or finish, creating long, shallow scratches.
  • Wire brushing: Use a wire brush to brush the furniture in the direction of the grain, creating a distressed, rustic look.

You can also use a combination of these techniques to create a unique distressed finish that is perfect for your cottage style home. When distressing furniture, it is important to be careful not to overdo it. You want to create a distressed look, not a destroyed look. A little bit of distressing goes a long way.

Distressed finishes can be applied to both painted and unpainted furniture. If you are distressing painted furniture, you will need to remove the paint before you can start distressing the wood. You can do this by using a chemical stripper or by sanding the paint off.

Antique hardware

Antique hardware is another key element of cottage style furniture. Hardware includes things like knobs, handles, hinges, and drawer pulls. Antique hardware can add a touch of charm and character to your furniture, and it can also help to create a unified look throughout your home.

  • Original hardware: If your furniture has its original hardware, that is the best option. Original hardware is often made of high-quality materials and has a unique design that you won't find on new hardware.
  • Reproduction hardware: If your furniture doesn't have its original hardware, you can find reproduction hardware that is made to look like the original. Reproduction hardware is typically made of high-quality materials and is designed to be faithful to the original design.
  • Vintage hardware: Vintage hardware is another option for adding a touch of charm to your cottage style furniture. Vintage hardware is hardware that is at least 20 years old. It can be found at antique stores, flea markets, and online retailers.
  • Mixed hardware: You can also mix and match different types of hardware to create a unique look. For example, you could use antique knobs on a dresser and reproduction hardware on a nightstand.

No matter what type of hardware you choose, make sure that it is in good condition and that it matches the style of your furniture. Antique hardware can help to add a touch of charm and character to your cottage style home.

Pastel colors

Pastel colors are a popular choice for cottage style furniture. Pastel colors are soft, muted shades of traditional colors, such as pink, blue, green, and yellow. They create a sense of peace and tranquility, and they can also add a touch of femininity to a space.

Some popular pastel colors for cottage style furniture include:

  • Baby pink: Baby pink is a soft, delicate shade of pink that is perfect for creating a romantic and charming look.
  • Powder blue: Powder blue is a light, airy shade of blue that is perfect for creating a beachy or coastal look.
  • Mint green: Mint green is a refreshing, cool shade of green that is perfect for creating a spa-like atmosphere.
  • Lavender: Lavender is a soft, soothing shade of purple that is perfect for creating a relaxing and peaceful space.

You can use pastel colors on all of your cottage style furniture, or you can mix and match pastel colors with other colors to create a unique look. For example, you could paint your dresser a soft pink and your nightstand a light blue. Or, you could paint your bed frame a classic white and add pastel-colored accents, such as a lavender bedspread and mint green throw pillows.

No matter how you choose to use pastel colors, they are a great way to add a touch of charm and personality to your cottage style home.

Floral patterns

Floral patterns are another popular element of cottage style furniture. Floral patterns can add a touch of charm, femininity, and romance to a space. They can also help to create a connection between the indoors and the outdoors.

Some popular floral patterns for cottage style furniture include:

  • Chintz: Chintz is a printed cotton fabric with a colorful, floral pattern. Chintz is often used for curtains, upholstery, and bedspreads.
  • Toile de Jouy: Toile de Jouy is a printed cotton or linen fabric with a pastoral scene, such as a shepherdess tending her flock or a couple walking in a garden. Toile de Jouy is often used for curtains, upholstery, and wallpaper.
  • Gingham: Gingham is a woven cotton fabric with a checkered pattern. Gingham is often used for curtains, tablecloths, and napkins.
  • Paisley: Paisley is a teardrop-shaped pattern that is often used in Indian and Persian textiles. Paisley is often used for upholstery, curtains, and rugs.

You can use floral patterns on all of your cottage style furniture, or you can mix and match floral patterns with other patterns to create a unique look. For example, you could upholster your armchair in a chintz fabric and your sofa in a toile de Jouy fabric. Or, you could use a gingham tablecloth on your dining table and a paisley rug in your living room.

No matter how you choose to use floral patterns, they are a great way to add a touch of charm and personality to your cottage style home.

Cozy and inviting

Cottage style furniture is all about creating a cozy and inviting atmosphere in your home. This means choosing furniture that is comfortable, functional, and stylish.

  • Comfortable furniture: Cottage style furniture is typically very comfortable. This means choosing pieces that are upholstered in soft fabrics and have plenty of cushioning. You should also choose pieces that are the right size for your space. You don't want your furniture to be too big or too small.
  • Functional furniture: Cottage style furniture is also very functional. This means choosing pieces that have multiple uses. For example, a coffee table with a built-in storage ottoman is a great way to save space and keep your living room tidy. Or, a daybed with a trundle bed underneath is a great way to accommodate guests.
  • Stylish furniture: Of course, cottage style furniture should also be stylish. This means choosing pieces that reflect your personal taste and style. If you love traditional furniture, you might choose pieces with classic lines and simple details. If you prefer a more modern look, you might choose pieces with clean lines and geometric shapes.
  • Personal touches: The best way to make your cottage style home cozy and inviting is to add personal touches. This means adding things that make you feel happy and relaxed. For example, you might add a few family photos to your mantel or hang a quilt that your grandmother made on your wall. You might also add some fresh flowers to your coffee table or light a scented candle in your living room.

By following these tips, you can create a cottage style home that is both cozy and inviting.
