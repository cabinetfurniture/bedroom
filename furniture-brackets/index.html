Variety of types

There are many different types of furniture brackets available, each with its own unique purpose and application. Some of the most common types include:

**Angle brackets:** These brackets are L-shaped and are used to connect two pieces of wood at a 90-degree angle. They are commonly used in the construction of cabinets, shelves, and other furniture pieces.

**T-brackets:** These brackets are T-shaped and are used to connect three pieces of wood together. They are often used in the construction of tables, chairs, and other furniture pieces that require additional support.

**Flat brackets:** These brackets are flat and are used to connect two pieces of wood that are parallel to each other. They are commonly used in the construction of drawers, shelves, and other furniture pieces that require a strong and durable connection.

**Shelf brackets:** These brackets are specifically designed to support shelves. They are typically made of metal and are available in a variety of sizes and styles. Shelf brackets can be either visible or hidden, depending on the desired look of the furniture piece.

In addition to these common types of furniture brackets, there are also a number of specialty brackets available for specific applications. For example, there are brackets designed for use with glass, brackets that are adjustable, and brackets that are made from decorative materials.
