Rust and corrosion resistance

Cast iron garden furniture is highly resistant to rust and corrosion, making it an ideal choice for outdoor use. This is due to the unique properties of cast iron and the protective measures taken during the manufacturing process.

  • Natural resistance:

    Cast iron is naturally resistant to rust and corrosion due to its high carbon content. The carbon in cast iron forms a protective layer of iron oxide on the surface of the metal, which prevents the iron from reacting with oxygen and moisture in the air.

  • Powder coating:

    Many cast iron garden furniture manufacturers apply a powder coating to the furniture during the manufacturing process. Powder coating is a type of paint that is applied as a dry powder and then cured with heat. This coating provides an additional layer of protection against rust and corrosion, as well as enhancing the appearance of the furniture.

  • Galvanization:

    Some cast iron garden furniture may also be galvanized, which involves coating the furniture with a layer of zinc. Galvanization provides excellent protection against rust and corrosion, as zinc is a highly corrosion-resistant metal. Galvanized cast iron garden furniture is particularly suitable for coastal areas or other environments with high levels of moisture.

  • Regular maintenance:

    While cast iron garden furniture is naturally resistant to rust and corrosion, it is important to perform regular maintenance to keep it in good condition. This includes cleaning the furniture with a mild detergent and water, and applying a protective coating or sealant periodically.

By following these simple maintenance tips, you can ensure that your cast iron garden furniture remains rust-free and corrosion-resistant for many years to come.

Prone to chipping and cracking

Cast iron garden furniture is generally very durable and long-lasting, but it is important to be aware that it is prone to chipping and cracking if not properly cared for. This is because cast iron is a brittle material, meaning it is more likely to break or crack under stress or impact.

  • Rough handling:

    Cast iron garden furniture should be handled with care to avoid chipping or cracking. This means avoiding dropping or bumping the furniture, and lifting it properly when moving it.

  • Extreme temperatures:

    Cast iron furniture can be damaged by extreme temperatures, such as very hot or very cold weather. This is because the metal expands and contracts with changes in temperature, which can put stress on the material and cause it to crack.

  • Rust and corrosion:

    Rust and corrosion can weaken cast iron and make it more prone to chipping and cracking. It is important to keep cast iron furniture clean and dry to prevent rust and corrosion.

  • Improper assembly:

    If cast iron furniture is not assembled properly, it can be more likely to chip or crack. This is why it is important to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully when assembling cast iron furniture.

By following these tips, you can help to prevent chipping and cracking in your cast iron garden furniture and ensure that it lasts for many years to come.
