When selling furniture at a car boot sale, it's important to price your items competitively. You should also be prepared to negotiate on price with potential buyers. It's also a good idea to bring a variety of items to sell, as this will increase your chances of making a sale.

Tips

Here are some tips for selling garden furniture in the UK:

Tip 1: Clean and repair your furniture before selling it.

Before you sell your garden furniture, take some time to clean and repair it. This will make your furniture look more appealing to potential buyers and help you get a better price for it. If your furniture is made of wood, you can use a mild detergent and water to clean it. If your furniture is made of metal, you can use a metal cleaner to remove any rust or dirt. You should also repair any damage to your furniture, such as loose screws or broken legs.

Tip 2: Take high-quality photos of your furniture.

When you're selling garden furniture online, it's important to take high-quality photos of your furniture. This will help potential buyers see the condition of your furniture and make a decision about whether or not to buy it. When taking photos of your furniture, be sure to use a well-lit area and take photos from different angles. You should also try to stage your furniture in a way that makes it look appealing.

Tip 3: Write detailed descriptions of your furniture.

In addition to taking high-quality photos, you should also write detailed descriptions of your garden furniture. This will help potential buyers learn more about your furniture and make a decision about whether or not to buy it. In your descriptions, be sure to include the following information:

  • The type of furniture (e.g., dining table, chairs, sun loungers, etc.)
  • The material the furniture is made of (e.g., wood, metal, plastic, etc.)
  • The condition of the furniture (e.g., new, used, excellent condition, good condition, fair condition, etc.)
  • The age of the furniture
  • The brand of the furniture (if applicable)
  • The dimensions of the furniture
  • Any other relevant information (e.g., whether or not the furniture is weather-resistant, whether or not it comes with cushions, etc.)

Tip 4: Price your furniture competitively.

When pricing your garden furniture, it's important to be competitive. You should research the prices of similar furniture on selling platforms to get an idea of what a fair price would be. You should also consider the condition of your furniture and the age of your furniture when pricing it. If your furniture is in good condition and is relatively new, you can ask for a higher price. If your furniture is in fair condition or is older, you may need to price it lower.
