Secondhand Furniture Horsham Buying Guide and Best Stores

Written by Christine Jan 12, 2024 · 13 min read

Welcome to your guide to finding secondhand furniture in Horsham. Whether you're moving into a new home, revamping your current space, or hunting for unique antiques, buying secondhand is an eco-friendly and budget-savvy way to furnish your home.

We'll cover everything you need to know about buying secondhand furniture in Horsham. From the best stores to tips for spotting quality pieces, we've got you covered.

Before we dive into the best stores, let's walk through some key tips for buying secondhand furniture. These will help you find the best pieces for your home and ensure a successful shopping experience.

Second Hand Furniture Horsham

Here are 7 important points to keep in mind when buying secondhand furniture in Horsham:

  • Inspect for quality
  • Check for damage
  • Consider style and function
  • Negotiate prices
  • Test before you buy
  • Ask about history
  • Be prepared to refurbish

Remember, buying secondhand furniture is all about the thrill of the hunt. With patience and a keen eye, you're sure to find unique and beautiful pieces that add character to your home.

Inspect for quality

When inspecting secondhand furniture for quality, there are a few key things to look for:

Construction: Check the overall construction of the piece. Is it sturdy and well-made? Look for solid wood construction, dovetail joints, and mortise and tenon joinery. Avoid pieces that are wobbly, have loose joints, or are made from particleboard or MDF.

Finish: Examine the finish of the furniture. Is it in good condition? Look for scratches, dents, or peeling paint. If the finish is damaged, you may be able to refinish the piece yourself or have it professionally refinished.

Upholstery: If you're looking at upholstered furniture, check the condition of the fabric. Is it clean and free of stains? Are there any rips or tears? If the upholstery is damaged, you may be able to have it reupholstered.

Hardware: Check the hardware on the furniture, such as handles, knobs, and hinges. Are they all present and in good working order? If the hardware is missing or damaged, you may be able to replace it yourself.

Taking the time to inspect secondhand furniture carefully before you buy it will help you avoid making a costly mistake. By looking for these key signs of quality, you can ensure that you're getting a piece of furniture that will last for years to come.

Check for damage

Once you've inspected the overall quality of the secondhand furniture, it's time to check for any damage.

  • Structural damage: Look for any signs of structural damage, such as cracks, splits, or warping. These types of damage can compromise the integrity of the piece and make it unsafe to use.
  • Surface damage: Check for surface damage, such as scratches, dents, or peeling paint. Surface damage can usually be repaired or refinished, but it's important to assess the extent of the damage before you buy the piece.
  • Upholstery damage: If you're looking at upholstered furniture, check for any stains, rips, or tears in the fabric. Upholstery damage can be repaired or reupholstered, but it's important to factor the cost of repairs into your decision.
  • Hardware damage: Check the hardware on the furniture, such as handles, knobs, and hinges. Make sure that all of the hardware is present and in good working order. If any of the hardware is missing or damaged, you may be able to replace it yourself.

It's important to be thorough when checking for damage, as even small issues can turn into big problems down the road. If you're unsure about the extent of the damage or how to repair it, it's best to consult with a professional.

Consider style and function

When choosing secondhand furniture, it's important to consider both the style and function of the piece.

  • Style: Consider the overall style of your home and the room where you plan to place the furniture. Do you want a piece that matches your existing décor, or do you want something that stands out as a unique accent? Secondhand furniture stores often have a wide variety of styles to choose from, so you're sure to find something that fits your taste.
  • Function: Think about the function of the furniture. What do you need it for? Do you need a sturdy dining table for family meals, or a comfortable sofa for relaxing in the living room? Make sure that the piece you choose is suitable for its intended purpose.
  • Size: Measure the space where you plan to place the furniture to make sure that it fits. It's also important to consider the scale of the piece in relation to the other furniture in the room.
  • Comfort: If you're buying upholstered furniture, be sure to sit on it before you buy it to make sure that it's comfortable. Pay attention to the support and cushioning of the piece.

Taking the time to consider the style, function, size, and comfort of a piece of secondhand furniture will help you make a purchase that you'll be happy with for years to come.

Negotiate prices

One of the best things about buying secondhand furniture is that you can often negotiate the price. Here are a few tips for negotiating prices at secondhand furniture stores:

  • Be prepared to walk away: Before you start negotiating, be prepared to walk away from the purchase if you can't agree on a price. This will give you the upper hand in the negotiation.
  • Do your research: Before you go to the store, do some research to find out what the going rate is for the type of furniture you're interested in. This will help you determine a fair price to offer.
  • Start low: When you make an offer, start low. You can always increase your offer if the seller is unwilling to come down to your price.
  • Be willing to compromise: Be prepared to compromise on the price. The seller may not be willing to come down to your original offer, so be willing to meet them halfway.

Remember, negotiating prices is a skill that takes practice. The more you do it, the better you'll become at it. So don't be afraid to haggle a little when you're buying secondhand furniture. You might just be surprised at how much you can save.

Test before you buy

Before you buy a piece of secondhand furniture, it's important to test it out to make sure that it's in good working order.

  • Sit in it: If you're buying a chair or sofa, sit in it to make sure that it's comfortable and supportive. Pay attention to the cushioning and the springs.
  • Open and close it: If you're buying a cabinet or dresser, open and close the doors and drawers to make sure that they work smoothly. Check for any signs of damage to the hardware.
  • Test the electronics: If you're buying an electronic item, such as a TV or stereo, test it out to make sure that it works properly.
  • Look for signs of wear and tear: Pay attention to the overall condition of the furniture. Look for any signs of wear and tear, such as scratches, dents, or stains. Make sure that the furniture is sturdy and well-made.

Taking the time to test out a piece of secondhand furniture before you buy it will help you avoid making a costly mistake. By checking for any potential problems, you can ensure that you're getting a piece of furniture that will last for years to come.

Ask about history

One of the great things about buying secondhand furniture is that it often comes with a story. Asking the seller about the history of the piece can give you a better understanding of its value and character.

Here are a few questions you can ask the seller about the history of the piece:

  • Where did the piece come from? This question can help you determine the age and origin of the piece. It can also give you some insight into the seller's taste and style.
  • Who owned the piece before? This question can help you learn about the history of the piece and how it was used. It can also give you some ideas for how to style the piece in your own home.
  • Why are you selling the piece? This question can help you understand the seller's motivation for selling the piece. It can also give you some insight into the condition of the piece.
  • Do you have any other information about the piece? This is a catch-all question that can help you learn anything else the seller knows about the piece. This information could include the age of the piece, the materials it's made from, or any repairs that have been done.

Asking the seller about the history of the piece is a great way to learn more about it and make an informed decision about whether or not to buy it. It's also a great way to start a conversation with the seller and build a relationship.

Be prepared to refurbish

When buying secondhand furniture, it's important to be prepared to refurbish it. Even if the piece is in good condition, you may want to make some changes to update its style or make it more functional.

  • Clean and repair: The first step in refurbishing secondhand furniture is to clean and repair it. This may involve dusting, vacuuming, or washing the piece. You may also need to make some minor repairs, such as tightening screws or replacing knobs.
  • Refinish: If the finish on the furniture is damaged or outdated, you may want to refinish it. This can involve stripping the old finish and applying a new one. You can also use paint or stain to change the color of the piece.
  • Upholster: If the furniture is upholstered, you may want to reupholster it. This involves removing the old fabric and replacing it with new fabric. You can choose from a wide variety of fabrics to match your taste and style.
  • Add new hardware: You can also update the look of secondhand furniture by adding new hardware. This may include knobs, handles, or drawer pulls. You can find a wide variety of hardware at most hardware stores.

Refurbishing secondhand furniture is a great way to save money and get a piece of furniture that is unique and stylish. With a little time and effort, you can transform a tired old piece of furniture into a beautiful new addition to your home.
