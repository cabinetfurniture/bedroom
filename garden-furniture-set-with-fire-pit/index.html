Material durability

Wicker is another popular choice for garden furniture sets with fire pits. Wicker is a type of woven material that is typically made from rattan, willow, or bamboo. Fire pit safety

Fire pit safety is a top priority when using a garden furniture set with a fire pit. Here are some important safety tips to keep in mind:

Choose a safe location for your fire pit.

Place the fire pit on a level, non-flammable surface, away from any overhanging branches or structures. Keep the fire pit at least 10 feet away from your house and other buildings.

Never leave a fire unattended.

Always stay close to the fire pit when it is lit, and never leave it unattended. If you need to leave the fire pit area, even for a short period of time, be sure to extinguish the fire completely.

Keep children and pets away from the fire pit.

Teach children and pets to stay away from the fire pit, and never allow them to play near it. The fire pit can get very hot, and even a small spark can cause serious burns.

Have a fire extinguisher nearby.

In case of an emergency, it is important to have a fire extinguisher nearby. Keep a fire extinguisher close to the fire pit, and make sure that everyone knows how to use it.

By following these safety tips, you can help to prevent accidents and ensure that you and your guests can enjoy your fire pit safely.

Fuel type

The type of fuel you choose for your fire pit will depend on your needs and preferences. There are three main types of fuel for fire pits: wood, gas, and propane.

  • Wood: Wood is the most traditional type of fuel for fire pits. It creates a natural and cozy ambiance, and it can be used to cook food. However, wood can be difficult to light and control, and it can produce smoke and ash.
  • Gas: Gas is a more convenient type of fuel for fire pits. It is easy to light and control, and it produces less smoke and ash than wood. However, gas can be more expensive than wood, and it is not as portable.
  • Propane: Propane is a portable type of fuel for fire pits. It is easy to light and control, and it produces less smoke and ash than wood or gas. However, propane can be more expensive than wood or gas, and it is not as widely available.

Once you have chosen a fuel type, you will need to purchase a fire pit that is designed for that type of fuel. You can find fire pits that are designed for wood, gas, or propane at most home and garden stores.
